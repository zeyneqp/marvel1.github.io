Loki

Year: 2021

"Loki" is a Marvel Studios series on Disney+ that follows the God of Mischief, Loki, after he creates a new timeline. Captured by the Time Variance Authority (TVA), he's tasked with fixing disruptions in time, exploring themes of identity and free will while navigating through various timelines and realities.

The Avengers

Year: 2012

"The Avengers" is a superhero film released in 2012, directed by Joss Whedon. It is based on the Marvel Comics superhero team of the same name. The movie brings together iconic Marvel characters including Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, Hulk, Black Widow, and Hawkeye. These superheroes join forces to combat the villain Loki, who seeks to conquer Earth .
Avengers: Age of Ultron

Year: 2015

"Avengers: Age of Ultron" is a superhero film released in 2015, directed by Joss Whedon as a sequel to the original "Avengers" movie. It is part of the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). The story revolves around the Avengers—Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, Hulk, Black Widow, and Hawkeye—as they work to stop an artificial intelligence named Ultron, created by Tony Stark.
Avengers: Infinity War

Year: 2018

"Avengers: Infinity War" is a superhero film released in 2018 and directed by Anthony and Joe Russo. It is a significant installment in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). The movie follows the Avengers, the Guardians of the Galaxy, and other Marvel superheroes as they face their most formidable foe yet, Thanos. Thanos, a powerful being seeking to gather all six Infinity Stones.
Avengers: Endgame

Year: 2019

"Avengers: Endgame" is a 2019 superhero film that concludes the Avengers' battle against the villain Thanos. The story involves time travel as the remaining Avengers seek to undo the devastating effects of Thanos' actions, leading to an epic final battle and a conclusion to the Avengers' storyline in the Marvel Cinematic Universe.
